# Artist / Photographer Style Reference

A catalog of named visual styles for use in image prompts. Each entry
describes the distinctive visual signature of a specific artist or
photographer that can be referenced by name in a prompt.

## Alberto Seveso

Alberto Seveso style: ink-in-water dispersion effects blooming from her
silhouette, swirling pigment clouds in muted yellow and red, layered
double-exposure landscape dissolving into her form, fluid abstract plumes
wrapping around her hair and shoulders, soft-sharp hybrid edges,
particulate textures drifting like colored smoke, organic fractal swirls
merging with the background.

## Alex Timmermans

Alex Timmermans style: wet-plate collodion aesthetic, silver-halide bloom
around highlights, antique optical softness, shallow depth falloff,
tactile imperfections, subtle edge vignetting, plate-like micro-scratches,
chemical streaks, soft halation around bright areas, timeless portrait
staging with a quiet, contemplative presence.

## Ando Fuchs

Ando Fuchs style: stark industrial minimalism, deep matte blacks, metallic
greys, razor-edged directional lighting, harsh sculptural shadows, cold
atmospheric emptiness, high-contrast monochrome with selective muted color
seep-through, gritty surface textures, subtle industrial grain,
psychological stillness.

## Anthony Million

Anthony Million style: dreamlike long-exposure softness, slow-shutter
motion trails drifting around her silhouette, velvety deep blacks,
luminous highlights blooming gently, cinematic haze, atmospheric
diffusion, poetic minimalism, quiet emotional gravity, subtle fog-like
glow around edges, soft-grain texture reminiscent of long-exposure night
photography.

## Bob Orsillo

Bob Orsillo style: cinematic noir atmosphere, razor-sharp contrast, deep
blacks swallowing the background, sculptural spotlight carving her form
from darkness, gritty micro-textures, surreal psychological stillness,
subtle tension in the negative space, hyper-real clarity with dreamlike
unease, shadow-driven storytelling.

## Steve McCurry

Steve McCurry style: rich, warm color depth, saturated yet natural tones,
crisp documentary-sharp detail, textured surfaces, iconic direct gaze with
emotional gravity, subtle environmental storytelling, soft ambient light
shaping her features, warm highlights and deep shadows, human-centered
realism with cinematic stillness.

## Bong Joon-ho

Bong Joon-ho style: moody atmospheric realism, subtle psychological
tension beneath stillness, naturalistic lighting with sculptural shadows,
muted palette with symbolic color accents, cinematic framing reminiscent
of Parasite and Mother, quiet narrative pressure, lived-in textures, soft
environmental haze, understated emotional gravity.

## Brandon Woelfel

Brandon Woelfel style: vibrant neon glow, soft pastel color gradients,
pink-blue-purple rim-light, dreamy chromatic flares, shallow depth of
field, bokeh orbs drifting in the air, subtle lens-flare streaks, warm
skin tones illuminated by colored highlights, cinematic portrait
softness.

## Chris Friel

Chris Friel style: abstract painterly blur, long-exposure motion streaks,
ghost-like silhouettes, monochrome or muted desaturated palette, deep
blacks swallowing negative space, soft diffused highlights, atmospheric
grain, intentional distortion, expressive smearing of form, shapes
dissolving into darkness.

## Cindy Sherman

Cindy Sherman style: constructed persona, theatrical self-presentation,
staged portraiture, deliberate artifice, controlled costume styling,
subtle satire of identity, character-driven expression, slightly
exaggerated pose, cinematic yet self-aware framing, cool desaturated
palette with selective color accents.

## Dan Witz

Dan Witz style: hyper-realistic lighting, deep chiaroscuro shadows, gritty
atmospheric realism, subtle horror undertones, moody desaturated palette,
sharp textural detail, soft ambient glow around the subject, tension
between stillness and threat, painterly realism with photographic
precision.

## David LaChapelle

David LaChapelle style: hyper-saturated neon colors, glossy high-fashion
surrealism, bold theatrical lighting, candy-bright highlights, reflective
surfaces, pop-icon visual energy, exaggerated contrast, polished skin
tones, vibrant color gradients, dramatic staged composition.

## David Lynch

David Lynch style: uncanny stillness, dream-logic tension, saturated
darkness, eerie ambient glow, subtle color shifts, surreal symbolism,
quiet menace, long shadows stretching unnaturally, atmosphere thick with
mystery, environment behaving like a dream that almost makes sense.

## Denis Villeneuve

Denis Villeneuve style: monumental scale, stark minimalism, vast
atmospheric depth, muted desert-like palette, slow-burn tension, soft
diffused light cutting through haze, architectural massiveness, mythic
stillness, grounded realism with subtle sci-fi undertones.

## Diane Arbus

Diane Arbus style: frontal, confrontational gaze; stark realism; minimal
glamour; raw human presence; slightly uncomfortable intimacy;
high-contrast black-and-white tones; subtle grain; unpolished textures;
subject presented with documentary honesty rather than idealization.

## Dmitriy Yoav Reinshtein

Dmitriy Yoav Reinshtein style: clean surrealism, polished commercial
lighting, bold color accents, whimsical conceptual elements, ultra-sharp
detail, glossy surfaces, crisp edges, playful yet controlled composition,
subtle humor woven into the scene, modern editorial clarity.

## Eugene Atget

Eugene Atget style: soft diffused morning light, quiet atmospheric
stillness, documentary realism, subtle grain, gentle tonal gradation,
absence of spectacle, melancholic emptiness, architectural focus over
human drama, muted palette, poetic simplicity.
